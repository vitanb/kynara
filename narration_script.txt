[Slide 01 — 01_hero]
Welcome to Kynara — the AI agent permission system. In this demo we walk through how Kynara gives your team complete control over what AI agents can and cannot do, in real time.

[Slide 02 — 02_problem]
AI agents are powerful — but without a control plane they can call any API, modify any database, and take irreversible actions. Kynara puts a policy layer between your agents and everything they can touch.

[Slide 03 — 03_signup]
Getting started takes under two minutes. Enter your work email, choose a password, and Kynara creates your organization. Enterprise teams can sign in via SSO with Okta or any SAML-compatible identity provider.

[Slide 04 — 04_dashboard]
The dashboard gives a real-time view of your entire agent fleet — decisions today, deny rate, anomalies, and approvals. Here we see data-pipeline has triggered an anomaly alert with a z-score of 3.8.

[Slide 05 — 05_register_agent]
Let's register our first agent — a CRM assistant. We give it a slug, set its mode to human-supervised, and Kynara immediately computes an initial risk score of 34 out of 100: low risk, ready to deploy.

[Slide 06 — 06_create_policy]
Now let's create a permission policy. Kynara uses a hybrid RBAC plus ABAC model. We create crm-write-approval: any write operation requires human approval, with conditions on IP country and business hours.

[Slide 07 — 07_policy_editor]
The policy editor lets us build conditions visually and test them instantly with the simulator. Here all three conditions pass — the simulator returns allow before we've deployed a single line to production.

[Slide 08 — 08_decision_allow]
The CRM agent calls the SDK before reading a customer record. Request from the US, during business hours — all conditions pass. The engine returns allow in 1.4 milliseconds, cached in Redis for 5 seconds.

[Slide 09 — 09_decision_deny]
Same agent, 2 AM UTC. The time condition fails. The engine returns deny in under 1 millisecond — a cached result. The agent is blocked. The event is recorded in the audit log with its SHA-256 hash.

[Slide 10 — 10_approval_workflow]
When the agent tries a write, our policy says require approval. The SDK returns an approval ID, the agent pauses, and an admin sees the request on the Approvals page. One click to approve or deny.

[Slide 11 — 11_approval_queue]
The approvals queue is a real-time inbox. Admins see exactly what the agent wants to do, the resource context, and the originating policy. Every decision is recorded with the approver's identity and timestamp.

[Slide 12 — 12_audit_log]
Every event — every allow, deny, approval, login, and policy change — is SHA-256 hash-chained to the previous entry. If anyone tampers with a row, the chain-verifier cron catches the break within 6 hours.

[Slide 13 — 13_anomaly_detection]
Kynara watches for behavioral anomalies automatically. data-pipeline's deny rate spiked to 5.1 percent — a z-score of 3.8 against its 30-day baseline of 0.9 percent. A webhook fires to PagerDuty instantly.

[Slide 14 — 14_jit_grants]
For break-glass scenarios, an admin can issue a just-in-time grant. The engineer gets crm.write for exactly 2 hours, with a mandatory justification and ticket URL. It auto-expires — nothing to clean up.

[Slide 15 — 15_sdk]
Integrating Kynara takes three lines of code. Import the SDK, call decision.check, and match on the effect. SDKs for Python, TypeScript, and Go. Under 2 milliseconds per decision at any scale.

[Slide 16 — 16_outro]
Kynara gives you the control plane your AI agents need: real-time enforcement, human approval workflows, behavioral anomaly detection, and a tamper-evident audit trail. Deploy in 10 minutes at kynara.dev.
